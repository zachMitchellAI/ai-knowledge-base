# AI Knowledge Base

It's early June of '26 and the AI industry is changing a whole lot as I'm writing this. There's a ton of noise as a result, voices swaying left & right as to where you need to go. As I've gone through this dance personally, this noise feels like there's radical opinions on what should be done with this very large tool we've been assembling.

This repository aims to be a place where it's possible to just *think* - and think in an educated way. Since it's early as of this writing, I've been picking up practices just on the fly without really having a stable place to put everything in. That ends with this repository, and hopeufully starts discussion, knowledge, and sharpening of what I know and are learning in real time!

# So what will this repository contain?

Great question! It'll have a little bit of everything, including:

* **Standards & guidelines** being learned in real-time
* **Slash commands & skills** actually being used by me
* **Stories** of things I've done
* **Terminology & Definitions** on aspects of AI, at least on how I understand it
* **Resources & popular voices** that arrive on either my youtube feed or RSS

# But like, why?

In honesty I just need a place to jot down things based on my existing understanding. It's never a good idea to just let things live in your head because:

* You'll probably forget something
* You might remember it but then the memory "bends" (modifies itself)
* There's never a feedback loop from an outside source (Other humans, AI)

You gotta move, if you can do that then progress will show itself, both to your own-self and to the surrounding area.

# Discussion, Contribution & Issues

Found something interesting (or terrible) and want to share your opinion? I'll be keeping a few places open in addition to the repo itself:

* **Discussions** - Forum to chat about all the things
* **Issues** - if you find a flaw in what's in the repo, whether that be a general practice, or perhaps a slash command that could be built better
* **Contributions** - if you're ambitious and want to put something in here yourself (I'm not expecting anything), fork this repo and submit a PR. This doesn't have to just be mine, it can be yours too.

# Hand-written vs Assistance Disclosure

Unless otherwise stated, the stuff I'm writing here will be hand-written. So in other words I'm typing this all out on either my framework 13's keyboard, or my EEPC Seashell from 2008 (or another fancy contraption!)

Sometimes things like slash commands, or guidelines may have a large language model modify the contents. This is because the models themselves might hit a snag when running through content, and they can write their own notes on how to mend the issue in the future for other models.
