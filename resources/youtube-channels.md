## Objective

People that are looking at the numbers & finances, asking "why". They may or may not like ai... but either way display a very blunt opinion and understanding.

### The Tech Report

![PFP](https://yt3.googleusercontent.com/ytc/AIdro_mgvWWfrtHCDfEFBqPEBgC2aHgj1qZcJsoRzkZ_BUY)

They are looking at the industry with the lense of finance & stocks. A lot of what I hear from them stems on data centers not being built, the business model behind OpenAI and Anthropic, and what would happen to companies like Oracle, should they choose to continue down this path of an alleged AI Bubble

His opinions are strongly towards "AI will fail" - big statement, but nonetheless shares big takes on the money side of it all.

https://www.youtube.com/@TheTechReportTR

### House of EI AI

![PFP](https://yt3.googleusercontent.com/bpGuxIt7WyRJNdJwxIpVCRj04UJbNb2J9I3Za7R_mbwnmddNKbKiqi18R5lbHvB_kp6_sDxNRg)

She is a software engineeer viewing everything objectively; a lot of what I hear from her is typically a double blend:

* Doom & gloom near the beginning
* How we can change things in the video midway (acknowledging she herself is an engineer usually)

She's worth mentioning because of the battle that she attemps to challenge - the public perception of AI. The content format is something I feel like is too strong of a hook for my taste, yet I'm not necessarily the exact audience either. If the goal is to hook-on people that are critisizing AI on the daily, then she does exactly this, while also sharing objective and also valuable perspectives on her own experience.

https://www.youtube.com/@houseofel-ai
