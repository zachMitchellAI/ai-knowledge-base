# AI

# Synopsis

## Modern-day term

A generative [model](./model.md) that is capable of outputting a form of media "creatively".

Examples that come to my mind:

1. [Large Language Models](./llm.md) (LLMs)
2. [Stable Diffusion](./stable-diffusion.md) Models
3. [Video Generation](./video-generation.md) Models
4. [Music Generation](./music-generation.md) Models

This repository primarly focuses on **LLMs**.

## Original Term

* **Artificial Intelligence** - the generic term used for projects created trough the practice of **Machine-Learning**, and **Deep Learning**
* (Video Games) The **CPU** - in other words, a hard-baked algorhythm for an opponent in the target video game. It can either be a dedicated bad guy, or an opponent in place of ahuman, such as common in fighter games, or racing games.

# Confusion

Marketing and buzzwords have caused this term to be what it is. It's an example of the errosion of what was originally one word, but it's caused global confusion to entirely other definition. Artificial Intelligence, and Machine Learning are real fields that are very cool in their own right, yet they may be mis-represented due to people thinking of the term in a completely different way.

I personally prefer to use the term **LLM** where possible, as it's more clear about what's being discussed.

Using "AI" when describing something can feel extremely loaded - at this point if you're talking to a professional, they'll understand with the right context. Yet, it still means a lot all at once if the context isn't there.
